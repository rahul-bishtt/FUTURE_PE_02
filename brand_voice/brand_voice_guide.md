# 🎙️ Brand Voice Guide — GlowLab
**Product:** Vitamin C Face Serum
**Platform:** Instagram Reels (UGC Ads)

---

## Brand Overview

**GlowLab** is an Indian D2C skincare brand that believes effective skincare shouldn't cost a fortune. The brand is rooted in science but speaks the language of real people — no jargon, no false promises, just honest results.

---

| Attribute | Details |

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. 🤝 Honest
GlowLab never overpromises. The content acknowledges real timelines (4 weeks, not 4 days), real skin concerns, and real results — no magic claims.

> ✅ *"4 weeks of consistent use — my dark spots are visibly lighter"*
> ❌ *"Erases dark spots overnight!"*

---

### 2. 💬 Conversational
GlowLab talks like a friend, not a brand. Scripts use everyday language, fillers like "yaar", "honestly", and contractions. It never sounds scripted or corporate.

> ✅ *"I was so wrong. Like genuinely embarrassed it took me this long."*
> ❌ *"GlowLab Vitamin C Serum delivers superior results."*

---

### 3. 🇮🇳 Indian & Relatable
GlowLab is proud to be Indian. The content references real Indian experiences — chai runs, Swiggy orders, college culture, Indian sun, desi skin tones.

> ✅ *"costs less than your weekly Swiggy order"*
> ✅ *"actually works under the Indian sun"*
> ❌ Generic Western skincare references that don't feel local

---

### 4. 📚 Credible Without Being Clinical
GlowLab educates — but simply. It explains *why* ingredients work in plain language, not in a way that sounds like a medical journal.

> ✅ *"Vitamin C reduces melanin — that's the stuff that causes dark spots"*
> ❌ *"Ascorbic acid inhibits tyrosinase enzyme activity to reduce melanogenesis"*

---

### 5. 💪 Empowering (Not Shaming)
GlowLab never makes people feel bad about their skin. The message is always: you deserve great skin, and it's accessible.

> ✅ *"This is your sign to try it"*
> ❌ *"Stop hiding your skin problems"* or *"Fix your bad skin"*

---

## Tone Across Content Types

| Content Type | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hooks | Bold, punchy, sometimes vulnerable |
| Scripts | Warm, personal, conversational |
| Captions | Relatable, occasionally playful, clear CTA |
| Ingredient posts | Educational but simple |
| Reviews / testimonials | Raw, authentic, unpolished |

---

## Words & Phrases TO USE ✅

- *"honest review"*
- *"yaar", "honestly", "genuinely"*
- *"my skin has never looked like this"*
- *"no filter"*
- *"Indian skin"*
- *"link in bio"*
- *"thank me later"*
- *"4 weeks", "30 days"* (specific timeframes)
- *"no harmful chemicals"*
- *"worth every rupee"*

---

## Words & Phrases to AVOID ❌

- *"luxurious"*, *"premium formula"* (sounds corporate)
- *"miracle"*, *"magic"*, *"instant results"* (false claims)
- *"flawless"*, *"perfect skin"* (unrealistic standards)
- *"anti-aging"* (not the brand's positioning)
- Heavy scientific jargon without explanation
- Negative body language: *"bad skin"*, *"ugly spots"*

---

## Posting Best Practices for Instagram Reels

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Video length** | 15–30 seconds (sweet spot for Reels) |
| **Lighting** | Natural light preferred — feels authentic |
| **Face on camera** | Always show the person's face — builds trust |
| **Filter usage** | Minimal or none — raw skin shots perform better |
| **Subtitles** | Always add — 80% of Reels watched without sound |
| **Post timing** | 7–9 AM or 7–10 PM IST (highest Indian engagement) |
| **Frequency** | 3–5 Reels per week for consistent reach |
